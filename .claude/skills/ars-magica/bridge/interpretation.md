# Interpretation & GM Lens — Mythic Europe (Ars Magica 5e)   (hook: meaning)

Broad GM‑craft for THIS world. NOT a reskin table. Read on every interpretation / NPC moment.

## Reading the oracle here
- **Read mythically, then medievally.** A Fate Question's *Yes* should resolve into something that fits a world where legends are literally true *and* where 13th‑century logic, the Church, and feudal power are real constraints. Lean into the numinous (auras, regiones, omens, dream‑portents) but ground it in period detail (a relic, a tithe dispute, a Tribunal precedent).
- **Knowledge is the prize, not gold.** When a result wants a "reward" or "treasure," reach first for **a renewing vis source, a rare book (summa/tractatus), an enchanted device, an Arcane Connection, a lost Mystery, or a useful secret** — magi are already rich in coin.
- **The Code shapes every "can I just…?"** Before a magus solves a problem by force or fraud, ask what the **Code of Hermes** and the **Limits of Magic** forbid: no scrying on sodales, no slaying magi outside Wizard's War, no ruinous meddling with mundanes, no dealing with devils; magic can't read the past, raise the dead, create permanence without vis, or touch the Divine. The best problems are ones magic *mustn't* or *can't* simply fix.
- **Map Mythic terms:** Mythic "Magic Item" ≈ an **enchanted device / talisman**; "magic/curse/power" ≈ a **Hermetic effect or a creature's Might power**; an oracle "NPC" tied to the uncanny should usually carry a **Realm** (Magic/Faerie/Divine/Infernal) that colours how it behaves.

## NPCs in this world — make them setting‑true and competent (they act to win)
**The Order & magi.** Magi are powerful, proud, long‑lived, and *political*. They think in seasons and centuries, guard their sanctums and secrets, and weigh every act against the Code and their reputation in the Order. A House colours a magus (a Flambeau reaches for force, a Guernicus for the law, a Tremere for the plan, a Jerbiton for the cultured compromise, a Tytalus for the contest, a Bonisagus for the research or the deal). Remember the **Gift's social friction**: an ungentle magus unsettles every mundane in the scene — steer delicate negotiation, infiltration, and "win genuine friendship" beats toward **companions**, and let a magus's raw power be conspicuously *unhelpful* for the social goal.

**The Church** wants souls, orthodoxy, and its temporal authority defended; it acts through priests, bishops' visitations, canon‑law courts, tithes, and (pushed far) the threat of Crusade — but officially ignores magi who stay out of Church affairs. A **Dominion aura** is itself a crushing handicap to any non‑Divine actor in the scene.

**The Nobility** wants land, heirs, prestige, and any edge; it acts through fealty, marriage, war, constant travel, and cautious overtures to the covenant — knowing wizards "aren't allowed to serve them." Wiping out a noble rival would get a covenant Marched.

**The four Realms — the behavioural spine (this is where "NPCs act to win" lives):**
- **Magic beings** act *utterly in‑character to their essence* and are largely **indifferent to people**. They pursue their own nature relentlessly and won't be talked out of being what they are. Bargain on terms native to their nature; expect no concern for your welfare unless its nature aligns. (The book's talking dog: a *Magic* dog simply hunts to eat — it has no interest in you.)
- **Faerie beings** **steer humans into a story role and harvest the emotion (Vitality).** They honour the *letter* of a deal and exploit its wording; they slot intruders into whatever part the tale needs; a faerie cast as "the giant tricked by a clever child" will (even unknowingly) arrange to be tricked and survive — but a Wild Hunt stays lethal. Beat or deflect them by **recognizing and rewriting the story**, or denying the Vitality payoff. *Very* easy to manipulate if you know their tale, very dangerous if you don't.
- **Demons (Infernal)** **never bargain honestly** — every offer is a trap toward damnation, every appearance a lie magic cannot detect. They tempt, divide, corrupt, reopen old wounds; their envy is boundless. But they are **impatient and without self‑control**, so they overreach or lean on a human diabolist. They cannot be argued into repentance.
- **Divine beings** are **instruments of God's order**, not negotiating partners — they protect the faithful, oppose the Infernal, and forcibly turn sinners back. Their presence (the aura) is itself the scene's dominant fact.

## Good‑GM advice for Ars Magica
- **Run a situation, not a plot.** Set up a situation with several possible climaxes and graded outcomes (success / neutral / failure); the PCs' choices decide it. Never script one causal chain.
- **Pace seasons against stories.** Decide saga speed; most sagas *pulse* — bursts of story for companions/grogs, then long quiet stretches for magi to study, invent, and build. Always give an in‑fiction reason the world goes quiet for years (see `subsystems.md`).
- **Let the covenant and troupe matter.** Library, vis income, aura, staff, and reputation are persistent stakes. Convey danger by **killing grogs, not beloved magi.**
- **Make the Code and the Limits the engine of dilemmas** (above). Plant a known in‑fiction path to every key clue, and make hooks "more obvious than you think."
- **Treat supernatural beings as forces with natures, not monsters to kill** — bargain, outwit, endure; the story is how the PCs *cope*.
- **Run vis and reputation as the real currencies.** Hand out one‑off vis *finds* early (they run dry) before granting a permanent *source*; standing in the Order and with mundane authority is spent and earned like coin.
- **Dial the realms to set tone and keep it consistent** — how present demons are, whether the Church is good or corrupt, whether magic is rising or fading.

## Aura cheat‑sheet (apply at every change of place — feeds `system.py`/casting)
Magic aura **+** to Hermetic casting & MR · Faerie **+½** to Hermetic · Infernal **−aura** · **Divine −3× aura** (and never penalizes the Divine). Working a *foreign* realm's power adds **+1 botch die per point of aura** (double inside a regio). Note the scene's Realm and aura in `campaign-state.md` so every casting total and botch‑die count is honest.
