## Premise / tone
The default year is **1220**. It is **Mythic Europe** — the 13th‑century Christian world as people of the time believed it to be, where the myths are *true*: faeries drink the milk left on doorsteps, demons walk the countryside, saints work miracles, and magi wield power beyond mortal ken. Tone: **mythic, scholarly, political, eerie, morally complex, historically grounded.** The unit of play is the **covenant** (a community of magi and their folk), not a lone hero. Stakes sit in the Code, vis, books, reputation, the Order, the covenant's survival, and entanglement with the supernatural and mundane authority — *not* usually in lethal combat.

## The Order of Hermes
A society of ~**1,200 magi** across **thirteen Tribunals**, founded **767** at Durenmar in the Black Forest. It exists because **Bonisagus** invented (1) the **Parma Magica**, which lets the Gifted meet without their Gifts repelling one another, and (2) a unified **theory of Hermetic Magic**; the sorceress **Trianoma** saw the Parma's promise and gathered the wizards. Twelve Founders + Trianoma swore the **Code of Hermes**; Houses Flambeau and Tytalus offered hedge wizards "**Join, or Die.**" The **Schism War** (~1000s) ended with **House Diedne** declared Renounced and destroyed (its leaders never found). By 1220 the last magi who remember the War have died or passed into Final Twilight.

### The twelve Houses
*True Lineages* (Bonisagus, Guernicus, Mercere, Tremere) · *Mystery Cults* (Bjornaer, Criamon, Merinita, Verditius) · *Societates* (Ex Miscellanea, Flambeau, Jerbiton, Tytalus). A magus belongs to **one** House.
- **Bonisagus** — masters of Magic Theory and Hermetic research; "Trianomae" politicians hold the Order together.
- **Bjornaer** — beast‑souled magi, each with a personal **heartbeast** animal form.
- **Criamon** — body‑marked mystics pursuing "the Enigma"; disdain raw power; act by alien logic.
- **Ex Miscellanea** — vast, disorganized House of hedge traditions; accepts almost any Gifted wizard.
- **Flambeau** — combat magi (mostly fire and destruction); the Order's warriors, with an honour strand.
- **Guernicus (Quaesitores)** — the Order's lawgivers and investigators of crimes against the Code.
- **Jerbiton** — magi of art, civilization, and the mundane world; brokers with nobility and Church; Gentle Gift common.
- **Mercere** — the messengers; Gifted and unGifted **Redcaps** who carry news and bind the Order together.
- **Merinita** — faerie‑bound magi who study and defend the fae; seek answers in Arcadia.
- **Tremere** — disciplined planners and strategists; invented Certámen; respect hierarchy.
- **Tytalus** — masters of every kind of conflict; thrive on struggle; once tainted by diabolism, since purged.
- **Verditius** — unmatched crafters of enchanted items; bound by their Founder's flaw to use casting tools; proud, vendetta‑prone.

### Covenants
A covenant is a group of magi (typically ~half a dozen, plus dozens–hundreds of mundane **covenfolk**: grogs, autocrat/steward, craftsfolk) who share a place, a **Magic aura**, a library, and a vis income, usually set apart from mundane settlements. *(The Spring/Summer/Autumn/Winter covenant life‑cycle is from the* Covenants *supplement, not the core book.)*

### The Code of Hermes (key clauses)
A magus will not: **deprive another magus of magical power**; **slay or attempt to slay** a fellow magus except by formally declared **Wizard's War**; **endanger the Order** through their actions; **interfere with mundanes so as to bring ruin upon their sodales** (serving as a court wizard has been ruled a violation); **deal with devils** (the one clause enforced absolutely); **molest the faeries** lest their vengeance fall on others; **use magic to scry upon members of the Order**. They **will abide by Tribunal decisions** and train apprentices who swear the Code. Breaking it can bring a **Wizard's March** (Renunciation): the offender loses the Code's protection and may be lawfully killed. Investigated by **Quaesitores**, often backed by **Hoplites**; judged at **Tribunal**.

### Tribunals
Regional **Tribunals** meet every **7 years** (quorum ≥12 magi from ≥4 covenants, one Quaesitor present; chaired by the eldest, the **Praeco**). The **Grand Tribunal** meets every **33 years** at Durenmar. The thirteen regions: **Rhine, Normandy, Provençal, Stonehenge, Loch Leglean, Hibernia, Greater Alps, Iberia, Rome, Theban, Transylvania, Novgorod, Levant.**

## The Gift & Parma Magica
**The Gift** is the rare capacity for magic (~1 in a few thousand). It carries a **social penalty**: bystanders instinctively distrust the Gifted (roleplay as a reputation for dishonesty/treachery) — **−3 to all social rolls** (the **Blatant Gift** is **−6**; the **Gentle Gift** has no penalty). Mundane animals shy away; the Gifted can't ride horses unaided. The Gift affects only those *in the presence* of the magus, and people get used to *specific* magi over time. **Parma Magica** (5 × Parma score to Form resistance) both resists magic and **negates the Gift's social effect among magi** — the foundation of the Order. **Demons are not bothered by the Gift.**

## The four Realms  (each aura modifies magic via the Realm Interaction Table — see `interpretation.md`)
- **Magic** — "the way the world could be": better versions of mundane things (beasts of virtue, elementals, spirits, ancient sites). Vivid, vis‑rich auras. Beings embody an **essential nature** and are largely **indifferent to humanity**.
- **Faerie** — beings drawn from **human stories**; they feed on **Vitality** when humans play out their tales; tricksters bound by their roles and bargains. Vis‑rich; auras run high. Split into Arcadia/Elysium/Eudokia.
- **Divine** — God's power; the **Dominion** around churches, ubiquitous and crushing to non‑Divine magic (**−3× aura to Hermetic casting**, never penalized itself). Angels, saints, relics, True Faith (MR = Faith ×10). Miracles cannot fail or be resisted.
- **Infernal** — evil and corruption; **demons are fallen angels**, beyond redemption, who **tempt, deceive, and damn**, envying humanity without bound; impatient, so they recruit **diabolists**. Infernal lies cannot be pierced by magic (only the Divine, and some faeries). **Infernal vis is shunned.**
A creature's **Might (Realm)** = its Magic Resistance; **regiones** are nested aura‑layers reaching toward a realm. **Raw vis** (measured in **pawns**: 10 = a rook) is magic stored in substances, each tied to one Art; a renewing **vis source** is a covenant's supreme treasure.

## Mundane society  (Ch.14) — frame ~1000–1400, officially 1220
- **The Church:** Christianity pervades life; backed by the **Divine**. Headed by the **Pope**; bishops, parishes, monasteries/convents, the new friars. Power: the **tithe**, property, canon law (bans usury), and the threat of Crusade. **Wants** souls saved, orthodoxy upheld, heresy/diabolism rooted out; officially ignores magi who keep out of Church affairs, but individual clergy range from "devil‑worshippers to destroy" to "fellow scholars." Could, with God's backing, destroy the Order — and both know it.
- **The Nobility / feudalism:** oaths of fealty trade land (fiefs) for military service; messy overlapping loyalties; inheritance son‑from‑father; nobles travel constantly and prize war, tournaments, the hunt. Magi are **forbidden to swear fealty or be court wizards**; relations are wary. **Wants** land, heirs, prestige, and any magical edge — pursued by marriage, war, and subtle overtures.
- **Towns & trade:** markets (weekly) and great **fairs** (Champagne) draw all Europe; towns are walled, crowded, foul‑aired, run by merchant councils, somewhat outside feudalism. Most magi avoid cities (Dominion auras; the Gift).
- **Peasants:** ~90% of people; nearly powerless, illiterate but not ignorant; the supernatural is ordinary village life; they fear magi but may accept "their" wizards as fair lords.
- **Women:** the era is patriarchal; women gain public power chiefly by absence/inheritance/widowhood/stewardship/becoming a nun — **but within the Order of Hermes women magi are treated as equals** (a position of equity from Platonic philosophy and the scarcity of the Gift).

## Hard content lines (default; confirm with the player at Session Zero)
- The setting's historical bigotries (misogyny, antisemitism, religious persecution) **exist in the world but are not celebrated**; portray their weight without gratuitous detail, and keep any specific lines the player names off the table.
- Demons tempt and corrupt — keep their evil's *texture* menacing, not gratuitously graphic.
